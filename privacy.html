Privacy policy

How Hallie for Animals collects, uses, and protects your information.

We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting any personal data you share with us. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect when you visit or use hallieforanimals.org (the “Site”), how we use it, and your rights in relation to that data.

By using the Site you agree to the practices described here. If you do not agree, please do not use the Site.


1. About HallieForAnimals


HallieForAnimals is an independent animal advocacy project. We are not a registered charity or nonprofit. This Policy applies to all visitors and users of the Site.


2. Information We Collect


We collect only the data that is necessary for the purposes described below.


2.1 Anonymity Guarantee

We guarantee total anonymity for all submissions (case reports, In Memoriam tributes, scam reports, general inquiries, tech issues, etc.). Submissions are anonymous by default. We do not associate your submission with any personal identity unless you voluntarily provide personal details (which are optional).

We will not voluntarily disclose your identity, the fact that you made a submission, or any details of your submission to any third party. The only exception is if we are legally required to do so by law (for example, a valid police search warrant, court order, or other legal obligation from law enforcement authorities). In such cases we will disclose only what is strictly required by law and will notify you if we are legally permitted to do so.


2.2 Information You Give Us

  1. Submissions (case reports, In Memoriam tributes, scam reports, general inquiries, tech issues): Name, email address, phone number, or any other personal details you voluntarily provide. All submissions are anonymous by default.
  2. Uploads: Photos, videos, or other files you attach to a submission. We do not store any metadata from these files.
  3. WhatsApp channel: When you join via the public link, we can see your phone number, display name, and status (as visible to channel administrators).
  4. Donations: When you donate via Stripe we receive your name, email address, and billing address directly through the Stripe platform.


2.3 Information Collected Automatically

  1. Website usage data: number of visits, clicks on Call-to-Actions, location (country/region level only), and device type. We do not log IP addresses.
  2. Spam-prevention data: Cloudflare Turnstile collects basic technical signals (e.g., browser characteristics) to distinguish humans from bots.


We do not collect or process any special category data (such as health data) beyond what is voluntarily included in a submission about animal welfare cases.


3. How We Use Your Information and Lawful Basis


We process personal data only where we have a lawful basis under GDPR and UK GDPR:

We never sell your personal data.


4. Sharing and Third-Party Processors


We never share any submitted personal data (names, emails, phone numbers, etc.) with third parties, law enforcement, rescues, or anyone else, with or without your consent.

We use the following processors who may have access to limited data:

  1. GitHub and Cloudflare – website hosting, content delivery, security, and analytics (location/device type only).
  2. Stripe – payment processing. Stripe sends automated thank-you emails using the email address you provide.
  3. Google Drive – embedded videos on campaign pages (Google may set its own cookies when the video loads).


All processors are contractually required to protect your data and act only on our instructions.


5. International Transfers


Some of our processors (Stripe, Google, Cloudflare, GitHub) are based in the United States. Where personal data is transferred outside the UK/EU, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses or the EU–US Data Privacy Framework (where applicable).


6. Data Retention


We keep personal data only as long as necessary for the purpose it was collected.

  1. Submissions and related personal details: maximum 7 days (deleted after review or if unused).
  2. Donation records: only as long as needed for our operational and legal record-keeping.
  3. WhatsApp channel data: only while you remain a member.
  4. Analytics data: retained in aggregated form; no personal identifiers.


We cannot guarantee exact deletion timelines in every case, but we delete or anonymise data as soon as it is no longer required.


7. Your Rights (GDPR)


You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  1. Right of access
  2. Right to rectification
  3. Right to erasure (“right to be forgotten”)
  4. Right to restrict processing
  5. Right to object
  6. Right to data portability
  7. Right to withdraw consent (where processing is based on consent)


To exercise any of these rights, please contact us. We will respond within 30 days.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner’s Office.


8. Children


The Site is intended for users aged 18 and over only (as stated in our Terms and Conditions). We do not knowingly collect or process personal data from anyone under 18. If we become aware that we have collected data from a child, we will delete it immediately.


9. Security


We use reasonable technical and organisational measures (including Cloudflare security features) to protect the personal data we hold. However, no system is completely secure.


10. Changes to This Policy


We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted here with the updated date at the bottom. Your continued use of the Site after the date of change constitutes acceptance of the revised Policy.


11. Contact Us


If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please contact us.
